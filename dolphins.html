About Dolphins

Dolphins are intelligent, social marine mammals known for their playful behavior and incredible abilities. They belong to the family Delphinidae and are found in oceans and rivers around the world.

Dolphin Species

Bottlenose Dolphin

The most well-known species, famous for their intelligence and friendly appearance.

Orca (Killer Whale)

The largest member of the dolphin family, known for their distinctive black and white coloration.

Amazon River Dolphin

Also known as the pink dolphin, this freshwater species is found in the Amazon and Orinoco rivers.

Conservation Efforts

Many dolphin species face threats from pollution, habitat loss, and fishing practices. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect these magnificent creatures.
